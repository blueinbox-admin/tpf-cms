bio:
  from: "Buenos Aires, Argentina"
qa:
  - question: |
      Dalia, tell us your Jewish story:
    answer: |
      Wow?  Where to start?  My whole life I went to a Jewish school here in Buenos Aires, called Shalom Aleichem.

      There I learned a lot about Jewish values, traditions and history.  (Until The Podcast Fellowship) everything I learned about Judaism was from there.
  - question: |
      How did your family get to Argentina?
    answer: |
      They were from Russia and Poland.  They wanted to leave Europe because of the issues there.  At the time, Argentina had a growing Jewish community.  Some of my family went to Entre Rios, which was a Jewish settlement, and the rest came to Buenos Aires.
  - question: |
      What kind of profession is your family involved in?
    answer: |
      My father is a lawyer, my mother is a dance teacher.  I am studying tourism.  In fact, I work for a Jewish organization called Menora.
  - question: |
      How did you hear about TPF?
    answer: |
      A friend of mine who now lives in Israel sent me a message about TPF.  She knows I love to learn about Judaism and we both love to practice our English, so it was the perfect combination!
  - question: |
      Tell us about your experience with TPF:
    answer: |
      It has gone very well.  I started about 2 months ago.  My podcast mentor is Ezequiel Chababo.  We discuss the podcasts for 20-30 minutes and he always has something to add, it's all very spontaneous, which I enjoy.  I have a lot of podcasts in my mind and I am growing.
  - question: |
      Have you ever done one-on-one learning before?
    answer: |
      No.  I have taken many Jewish classes in a group, but never this "chavruta" learning.  I enjoy how personalized this kind of learning is.
  - question: |
      What did you think about the offer of a stipend for discussing podcasts?
    answer: |
      I don't do the podcasts specifically for the money, because where I work I receive a salary.  TPF is good enough that it doesn't depend on the money, but of course, the money helps!
  - question: |
      What are some of your favorite topics to listen to?
    answer: |
      Wow, I have a lot.  I love podcasts that speak about ourselves and our Jewish identity.  My favorite podcast is "Why Be Jewish?" by Rabbi Orlofsky.  It was the first one I heard, and it shocked me and made me love TPF.
  - question: |
      How has TPF helped you grow?
    answer: |
      One way is that I always had a question about why Judaism pushes away people who are interested in converting, and I have never heard a compelling explanation. I recently heard a podcast on this topic which made the issue so clear to me.  Now I feel I can explain it to my friends who want to become Jewish.
  - question: |
      Do you have any friends in Buenos Aires that also do TPF?
    answer: |
      Yes, I have my own club!  Here at Menora we are telling everyone about The Podcast Fellowship!  There are at least 6 other students I recruited to TPF.
