  - question: |
      Brandon, have you experienced antisemitism on campus as of late?
    answer: |
      My campus wasn’t political about the Middle East before October 7th. It was like a light switch that turned on after Oct. 7th. I started to see pro-Palestine flyers all over campus after the terrorist attack on Israel.

      I headed down the advocacy route and became the voice for the Jewish students on the Buffalo campus. I hosted a peaceful Israel March, education session, vigil, and united all the Jewish organizations on campus.

      Because of my outspoken stance on Israel. I got harassed by a fellow student on 3 separate occasions. On one occasion, he saw me at the Pro-Palestine protest and pointed me out. He shouted at me and my friend and called us “Nazis, Fascists, and Kikes.”  And told me “go back to your grandma’s homeland….”

      A week later, he took a photo of me and created a flyer of me that said “'Palestians are insects…' -this Zionist Child”  He put the flyer all over campus. Antisemitism definitely increased on my campus and there were 4 Palestinian protest on my campus in one semester. I felt like the school was divided between pro- Israel and pro-Palestine students and faculty.

      I started to put on Tefillin after the October 7th attack.  Thanks to my Chabad rabbi, I got my own pair of tefillin and I started to wear my kippah around campus.
