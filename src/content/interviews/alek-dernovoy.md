  - question: |
      Alek, please share your Jewish story with us.
    answer: |
      My parents grew up in the Soviet Union.  After the Russian revolution, my family appeared outwardly as Christians to get by, but actually clung to Judaism.  My mother’s father's family was originally from Iraq, his great-grandfather fought for Ottomon Empire.  He ended up as prisoner of war in Russia, and sat in the gulag in Kumchoka, almost in Arctic Circle, for 5 years, for being religious.  When he was in the gulag, the state wanted to take all 8 of his kids and put them up for adoption, but the Jewish community acted quickly and all the kids were sent to different friends and relatives before the government officials arrived.

      I was born in Germany as my parents left the USSR, which was collapsing at the time.  We eventually immigrated to New York, and then to Portland to be close to family.

      In America, my parents struggled with freedom.  The community around them wasn’t observant of Judaism, so my upbringing was kind of inbetween Christianity and Judaism.  To me, Christianity didn’t make sense, so it wasn’t an issue to dive into Judaism.

    answer: |
      Last August I went to Israel for the first time with Birthright.  The experience helped me with my Jewish identity, and afterwards I was more comfortable with who I am.  I realized my story wasn’t unique, that many my age are struggling with Jewish identity.

      Back at home, I moved forward: I tried visiting shul every Shabbos.  Around the same time I discovered Podcast Fellowship on my Instagram.
