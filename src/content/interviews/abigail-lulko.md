  - question: |
      Abigail, how has your life been impacted by the massacres of October 7th and the increase of vocal antisemitism around the world?
    answer: |
      After October 7th, it felt like everyone started becoming more observant and closer to God.   For me it was the complete opposite; I was angry and did not understand why God would do this.  Seeing such a unity of the Jewish people was great, but on the other hand I didn’t understand why it took such a tragedy to create the unity.   It’s still hard for me to process October 7th because of my love for Israel.   In May of this year I went on Birthright and to the Nova Music Festival vigil.  I am in a college environment where I see antisemitism, which is hard, but I think surrounding myself with Jews and also making time to listen to The Podcast Fellowship has helped me with my relationship with God.

  - question: |
      Tell us about your experience with TPF:
    answer: |
      Every single podcast I listened to so far has been great.  I learn so much from them, and I also take notes so I can discuss it with my mentor Tzipora Portowicz (of Exhibit J Staten Island)  Notes are also helpful if I ever want to go back to reread what I wrote.  Tzipora is a great mentor; she listens to what I learn, I ask her questions and we build a nice discussion.   When the high holidays were coming up, she recommended I listen to a podcast about Elul/ Rosh Hashana & Yom Kippur.    Those podcasts helped me with a better understanding of those holidays.
  - question: |
      Who is your favorite podcast speaker and/or topic?
    answer: |
      My favorite topic that I listened to so far was “Understanding Suffering and the Purpose of Life” by Mrs Batya Burd, because she discusses her upbringing and how she thought life was only about money, eventually recognizing that life is about getting closer to God.  She talked about something tragic she went through and how she persevered and stayed close to God.  It was very inspirational.  Mrs Jackie Engel is my favorite speaker because she keeps the podcast interesting in the way she talks to her students in class.  In one of her podcasts called “Torah and Psychology” she talks about a lot of things that most people would probably be interested in,  like love, fear, anxiety and how to deal with it.
  - question: |
      How has TPF helped you grow as a Jew?
    answer: |
      I never went to a Jewish school, so listening to these podcasts gives me some knowledge that I can take with me.  I really learn a lot from them.  Also, once I speak to my mentor about a podcast and ask her questions, I get a deeper understanding of a topic.
  - question: |
      Where are you in your Jewish growth right now?
    answer: |
      I am more spiritual and love learning Torah.  This change happened since I started The Podcast Fellowship.  I am involved in different things, like Exhibit J in Staten Island and Oorah etc., but with TPF, I can just pick any topic I feel like learning that day and it’s great.
  - question: |
      Abigail, can you sum up your experience with The Podcast Fellowship in one quote?
    answer: |
      "The Podcast Fellowship makes me feel like there are other people that are similar to me; I’m not the only one that either struggles with my Judaism or did not have a observant Jewish upbringing."
