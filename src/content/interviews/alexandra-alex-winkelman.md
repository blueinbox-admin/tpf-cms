  - question: |
      What's it like being Jewish on a college campus nowadays?
    answer: |
      It can definitely be a bit scary, especially when you read about all these things that are happening on social media on different college campuses.

      Personally, at the U of A, it hasn't really been an issue.  And we've actually had great community support in the Jewish community at U of A, which has been really nice.  There have been a few protests for Students for Justice in Palestine, which have been fairly upsetting, but I would say as a whole, U of A has done a really good job of making all their students feel safe, and I really appreciate that.

      But scrolling through Instagram and getting all these WhatsApp messages can be really scary.
  - question: |
      Alexandra, what's your Jewish story?
    answer: |
      I was raised in a Conservative household; both my parents are Conservative Jews.  All of my grandparents are from Poland.  Everyone came over during the Russian pogroms of the late 1800's, so we've been in America a long time.

      I grew up going to Hebrew school every Sunday.  I had a bat mitzvah.  I would go to Camp Ramah in California every summer; I absolutely loved it.  I went to Israel for a gap year with Netiv, and now I'm president of the OLAMI chapter of U of A.
  - question: |

      Rabbi Weiss of OLAMI at U of A told me about it.
  - question: |

    answer: |
      To be honest, it seemed like an easy $15, and as a broke college student, it's very helpful.

  - question: |
      Tell us about your experience with TPF:
    answer: |
      I really like meeting with my mentor, Risa Brumer, every week.  Discussing the podcasts with her is a fun part of my week.  I feel like I learn a lot and they're usually positive.  I definitely enjoy being able to talk about something more positive and less stressful than school (laughs).

      I listen to a lot of different podcasts; I don't have a specific type that I listen to.
  - question: |
      Who is your favorite podcast speaker and/or topic?
    answer: |
      The very firsts podcasts I listened to were the 'Habits of Happiness' course.  I think it's a 7-episode course.

      I really liked that course; it wasn't too intense of a podcast and it gave me insight I could apply to my everyday life.  I also thought that it provided a lot of insight into how Judaism can relate to a topic that you don't necessarily think is Jewish, but then, once you start learning, you realize how much it actually does connect.
  - question: |
      How has TPF helped you grow as a Jew?
    answer: |
      TPF has helped me grow in that I've learned how every little thing in my life is connected to Judaism, even the things that I wouldn't think are (connected).

      When I'm with my friends, sometimes something will happen and I say, "oh my gosh!  I just listened to a podcast about this!" and they say, "oh my gosh--not again, Alex!"

      I've learned that there's so much that happens in my life that is connected Jewishly that I wouldn't have thought about before.
  - question: |
      Where do you stand in your Jewish growth?
    answer: |
      I still consider myself a Conservative Jew.  I've definitely grown a lot Jewishly in the last two years, some from The Podcast Fellowship, some from my gap year in Israel.  I now keep kosher (for the most part).  I don't do any homework on Shabbat.  I go to Shabbat dinner every Friday.

      The podcast is a good way to remind me weekly of these Jewish values, so I think it will help me continue to grow.
  - question: |
      Thank you, Alex!   Can you sum up your experience with The Podcast Fellowship in one quote?
    answer: |
      "As a college student, I'm very busy.  These podcasts remind me that you're never too busy to learn about Judaism."
