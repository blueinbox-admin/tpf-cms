qa:
  - question: |
      Daniel, what's your Jewish story?
    answer: |
      I was raised in a religious house; kosher, shomrei Shabbos. We go to shul every weekend.  My dad goes to shul every morning and afternoon and I try to go when I can.  I try to go a shiur on Shabbos afternoon, when I can.
  - question: |
      How did you hear about The Podcast Fellowship?
    answer: |
      Rabbi Michael Vegoda (of OhrSom Johannesburg) told me about it.  He used to be my next-door neighbor.
  - question: |
      What was your reaction to the stipend offer?
    answer: |
      Honestly, it seemed like an easy way to earn money.  Also, my father always wants me to learn more Torah.  So I saw this as a way to earn money and make my dad happy.
  - question: |
      Would you theoretically have done TPF without the stipend?
    answer: |
      Honestly, I don't think I would have.
  - question: |
      Tell us about your experience with TPF:
    answer: |
      Rabbi Vegoda is my mentor.  It's quite nice because although I'm in Durban and he's in Jo-berg (about 6 hours away by car),  whenever I'm in Jo-berg we do make our podcast session face-to-face.  Instead of having the half-an-hour discussion, we'll speak for an hour or so, which I find quite interesting.
  - question: |
      I don't exactly have a favorite speaker or topic, but I am generally more interested in questions that relate to myself, such as, "why must I put on tzitzit" or "why must I put on tefillin?
    answer: |
      A lot of the time our discussion will go over the time we're meant to spend but I don't really mind, because the stuff that I'm learning I can use straight away, as opposed to (topics about which I am likely to say), "ok, MAYBE I'll figure out how to use this, one day."
  - question: |
      How has TPF helped you grow as a Jew?
    answer: |
      It's given me a much better understanding for simple (foundational) things, like why do I need to daven; the importance of certain aspects of our religion.
  - question: |
      Where do you stand in your Jewish growth?
    answer: |
      I was already shomer Shabbos and kosher, but the odd thing with me is that although I kept kosher, I didn't really care; it wasn't so important.  Through the discussions with Rabbi Vegoda I realize it's a lot more important.  And now when I daven, instead of just rushing through it, I try to say it a lot slower (although sometimes I do still rush through it).  But TPF has definitely helped me realize that you don't have to rush through religion.  You can take your time and find ways to enjoy it.
  - question: |
      What's it like being Jewish on a university campus nowadays?
    answer: |
      I don't know.  All of courses have been online, luckily.  But in Durban I have seen antisemitism, mainly when driving behind cars with stickers on them.
  - question: |
      Thank you, Daniel!   Can you sum up your experience with The Podcast Fellowship in one quote?
    answer: |
      "An easy way to connect with G-d, while making money."
