  - question: |
      What kind of Jewish home did you grow up in, and what kind of Jewish education did you receive?
    answer: |
      I grew up in an orthodox/traditional Jewish home. Recently I have become a ba'al teshuva and started keeping Shabbat.  My mother is the head of Jewish life at my primary school, and traditions such as going to shul Friday and Saturday were always important to us.
  - question: |
      What motivated you to start keeping Shabbat?
    answer: |
      Was it difficult to begin keeping Shabbat, considering that you are still living with your parents?

      We always lived about 30 minutes away from my Sephardi shul.  There was a shul close by, but it was an older community, an Ashkenzi shul and I really feel connected to the nussach of the Sepharadim.  People my age don't usually go to shul, but I always went to shul, even at 12, 13, 14 years old; it was important to me, I really enjoyed it.  The Sephardi shul is in a neighborhood called Sea Point.  I always thought, "if I move to Sea Point I would probably keep Shabbat."  My parents actually recently moved to Sea Point.  I was very lucky to have a friend who keeps Shabbat, I tried one Shabbat and I immediately fell in love with the essence of Shabbat.

      My family b'ezrat Hashem will start keeping Shabbat soon.  My parents are Baruch Hashem the most supportive parents ever.  Even though they are not keeping Shabbat, they bought me a hot plate for my birthday, they got me an urn, tissue paper that I wouldn't have to rip...they really try to help me to make it easier.  They respect me.  I'm really really blessed to have supportive parents.
