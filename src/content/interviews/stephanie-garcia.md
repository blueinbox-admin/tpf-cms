  - question: |
      Tell us about your experience with TPF:
    answer: |
      My experience with the Podcast Fellowship has been really fun and insightful. I listen to a podcast every week and then meet with my mentor, Rachel Minchen (of OLAMI Souled), to discuss it. I found that if I just listened to the podcast, I would forget a lot about it after a few hours, so I started taking notes while listening, which forced me to actively listen.  It has been such a great experience; I have been able to learn and remember so much more.

      Additionally, I refer to my notes when I meet with my mentor, and I sort of “teach” her what I learned from the podcast, which further helps reinforce what I learned and sparks interesting, engaging discussions.

      Rachel is an incredible mentor who has helped me discover more about what I want to gain and learn from being Jewish and different ways to deepen my connection with Hashem. She is part of the reason I enjoy this fellowship so much!

  - question: |
      How has TPF helped you grow as a Jew?
    answer: |
      I went to public school for middle and high school, which obviously did not include Judaic studies. While I grew up keeping Shabbat and kosher, my Jewish education was limited to what I would learn at home and from programs such as JSU (Jewish Student Union, a program of NCSY).

      While I am immensely grateful that my parents raised me in a Shomer Shabbat and kosher home and have instilled so many wonderful values in me, I’ve always felt like I have been a passive observer; I mostly did it because it is what I grew up doing; I did not put much thought into it. This year, I decided I wanted to learn more and connect on a deeper level, rather than doing everything passively.

      There are so many topics in the Fellowship that I had never learned about before or had minimal knowledge about. TPF has helped me feel more passionate about being a Jew and more aware when it comes to my observance.  I feel that it has helped me become a more “active” Jew. It has also inspired me to connect more with prayer, which is something I don’t typically set time aside to do, but I want to start.

      Additionally, we've learned about modesty, another topic I was really interested in learning about more.  Ever since I started learning about modesty with my mentor, I have felt more proud and comfortable dressing modestly.

      The Podcast Fellowship is one of my main sources of Torah learning during the week as it is very easy to fit into my schedule. I have learned so much from TPF's podcasts and the discussions with my mentor.
