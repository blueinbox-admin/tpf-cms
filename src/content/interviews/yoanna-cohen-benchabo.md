  - question: |
      Yoanna, can you tell us your family's Jewish story?
    answer: |
      I proudly come from a traditional Sephardic family, with roots deeply embedded in Moroccan Jewish heritage.  My ancestors come from various parts of Morocco, including Tangier, Alcazarquivir and Oujda.  On my mother's side, the family eventually settled in Spain, while my father's side moved to France; both left Morocco after the establishment of the State of Israel.  My Moroccan background shapes almost every aspect of my day-to-day life, from values to customs.  It's a rich a beautiful culture that I carry with immense pride.
  - question: |
      What kind of Jewish home did you grow up in, and what kind of Jewish education did you receive?
    answer: |
      I was raised in a modern Orthodox, traditional home where Jewish values were not only observed but deeply cherished.  We are Shomer Shabbat, observe the holidays and keep kosher.  Judaism is lived with both pride and love in our home, which extends to our extended family as well.
  - question: |
      How did you hear about The Podcast Fellowship?
    answer: |
      I heard about it from my friend, Sol Simha Bensabat, who I grew up with and who now lives in Israel.  We both felt the same yearning for a spark of inspiration in our Jewish lives, and she encouraged me to apply.
  - question: |
      Would you have been interested in TPF without the $15 stipend offer?
    answer: |
      The stipend certainly helps maintain consistency and discipline, but it wasn't the reason I joined.  I was truly in search of something that would spiritually and personally inspire me--a framework to deepen my Jewish journey through thoughtful and relevant conversations.  TPF offered exactly that.
  - question: |
      Tell us about your experience with TPF.
    answer: |
      One of the most meaningful aspects of my experience has been my connection with my mentor, Goldie Abraham (of Olami Souled).  She has not only guided me through the Fellowship but has genuinely helped me grow.  We usually follow a weekly rhythm: I listen to a podcast, reflect on it by writing down my thoughts, and then present those reflections to her.  Our discussions are rich and layered, with each of us bringing our own perspectives.  I feel incredibly grateful for her mentorship and the depth of our conversations.
  - question: |
      Can you give some examples of how the discussions with Goldie help you grow?
    answer: |
      Yes.  Our discussions opened my mind about marriage and dating (topics to which my mind was closed off beforehand).  Also, we discussed many podcasts about how to love myself and about understanding the point of existence.  I've learned how to believe in G-d a bit better and understand better why believing in G-d is everything.
  - question: |
      What is your favorite podcast topic or favorite speaker?
    answer: |
      I am currently exploring the theme of Personal Development and Growth, which resonates with me deeply.  My favorite speaker so far has been Lord Rabbi Jonathan Sacks.  His wisdom continues to move and inspire me.
  - question: |
      Where do you stand in your Jewish observance today?
    answer: |
      Today, I consider myself a traditionally observant and proud Jew.  Being a student abroad, I am fortunate to have a strong and welcoming Chabad community here in Budapest, which helps me stay grounded and connected.

  - question: |
      Have you experienced increased anti-Semitism since October 7, 2023?
    answer: |
      Life has not been the same since October 7.  Emotionally and spiritually, I think many of us feel forever changed.  I have witnessed antisemitism--not directly towards me, but in many forms around me.  At first, I felt compelled to speak up and take a stand.  But as the situation worsened, and given that I live alone as a student, I've found myself keeping a lower profile in public spaces.  It's a difficult and painful balance between courage and self-protection.
  - question: |
      Yoanna, can you sum up your experience with The Podcast Fellowship in one quote?
    answer: |
